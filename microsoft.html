Takeaways

What Fortune 100 Scale Teaches You

Process Is Strategy

At Microsoft, the gap between a good idea and a result was usually a broken process. I stopped thinking about strategy as a plan and started thinking about it as a system. The 64% improvement in request processing time wasn't a creative breakthrough. It was constraint identification and workflow redesign.

Buy-In Is Not Optional

You can design the perfect system and have it fail completely because the people executing it weren't part of building it. Training 200+ employees during a merger taught me that communication and change management are not soft skills. They are the difference between a rollout that works and one that quietly dies.

Data Beats Intuition at Scale

At the scale Microsoft operates at, gut feelings don't move the needle. The A/B testing program I launched was built on a simple premise: stop assuming you know what works and start finding out. That mindset has shaped how I approach marketing decisions at every company since.
