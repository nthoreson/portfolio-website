Go-To-Market Strategy

Built for a market most marketers don't understand.

Web3 is a different animal. The buyers are different, the channels are different, and the trust signals that move people are completely different. Most traditional marketing playbooks fall flat in this space. I built DropChain's GTM from first principles.

Market Positioning

ICP and Messaging Built on Real Signals

I developed DropChain's full ideal customer profile and messaging framework by mapping the actual pain points of blockchain-native brands, the gap between engagement metrics and real audience relationships. The positioning focused on what brands actually needed: CRM infrastructure that understood wallet behavior, not just clicks.

Channel Strategy

Distribution That Actually Reaches Web3 Audiences

Getting in front of Web3 audiences means going where they actually are. I built a distribution strategy across native crypto channels, targeted content plays, and community-driven growth mechanisms designed to build trust with an audience that's allergic to traditional advertising.

Token Launch Support

Marketing Collateral for Investors and Partners

The GTM strategy extended into DropChain's token launch, covering the investor pitch collateral, partner-facing materials, and the full suite of assets needed to build credibility in a space where perception is everything. The brand went on to support enterprise partnerships and a product suite used across the Web3 ecosystem.

Results

Nearly 3 Million Engagements

The campaigns and content system I built drove nearly 3 million engagements across the platform's target audiences. That's not a vanity metric, it's what happens when your positioning is right, your distribution matches your buyer, and your content system is actually built to scale.

AI Automation

The Marketing Agent Waterfall

Most AI marketing tools are wrappers around a prompt box. This is different. I built a fully automated marketing system using Claude Code, a waterfall of 20+ specialized agents that conducts research, builds strategy, and executes content end-to-end without a human in the loop.

How It Works

Four stages. Fully automated.

Each stage is handled by a dedicated cluster of agents. They pass context downstream, build on each other's outputs, and execute without manual intervention. The result is a marketing system that runs on autopilot, producing research-backed, strategically-aligned content at scale.

01

Research

The waterfall starts with a cluster of research agents that map the competitive landscape, identify audience signals, and surface the insights that everything downstream depends on. No assumptions. No shortcuts. The agents pull real data before anything gets built.

Competitor Analysis Audience Research Trend Mapping Signal Detection
02

ICP & Messaging

Research outputs feed directly into a messaging agent that constructs the full ideal customer profile and messaging document. This isn't a template, it's a synthesized strategy document that defines who we're talking to, what they care about, and exactly how to position the product against their alternatives.

ICP Definition Positioning Framework Messaging Hierarchy Value Proposition
03

SEO Strategy

A dedicated SEO agent cluster takes the ICP and messaging output and builds a full keyword strategy and content calendar. It identifies the highest-value topics, maps them to buyer intent, and structures a publishing plan designed to compound over time, not just rank for a week.

Keyword Research Content Calendar Intent Mapping Topic Clustering
04

Content Execution

The final stage is where the system earns its keep. Content agents take the SEO strategy and produce publish-ready output across formats, blog posts, social content, email sequences, and more. The writing is aligned to the messaging doc from stage two, grounded in the research from stage one, and calibrated to the keyword strategy from stage three.

Blog Posts Social Content Email Sequences Autopilot Publishing
20+ specialized agents working in sequence. Research feeds strategy. Strategy feeds content. The whole system runs without a human writing a single word.
