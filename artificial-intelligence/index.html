02 / Data Analysis

Turning Client Data into Renewal Revenue

At Lean Marketing, clients were coming up for renewal without a clear way to see the value they'd built over the program. I built an AI system that analyzed every piece of engagement data we had on each client—coaching call transcripts, client health scores, milestone wins, support interactions, session attendance, and sentiment signals across the entire relationship—and synthesized it into a single engagement report. We called it holding up the mirror. Clients could see a full account of everything they'd accomplished, and advisors could walk into renewal conversations grounded in evidence rather than instinct. The result was a renewal rate that climbed from 19% to 70%.

03 / Content Creation

Calls In, Content Out

The best marketing content for a business is usually buried inside it. At Lean Marketing, I built a content pipeline that starts with client coaching calls and ends with finished drafts. Call recordings are transcribed and run through a prompt pipeline that identifies key insights, client wins, and teachable moments from the conversation. Those outputs are routed into LinkedIn posts, email sequences, and blog drafts—each written in a trained voice built from existing content examples. What used to take hours of manual writing now takes minutes, and the content is more grounded because it comes directly from real client conversations rather than starting from a blank page.

04 / Automation

One Source of Truth for the Whole Team

Most teams have data spread across five tools and no reliable system keeping any of it in sync. At Lean Marketing, I built a set of automations that created a single source of truth the whole team could trust. When a new client onboards, their profile is built across HubSpot, Notion, and internal tracking automatically—no one has to move data by hand. When a coaching session is completed, notes flow into the right place. When a client hits a milestone or their health score changes, the advisor gets a notification and the record updates. The team stopped chasing spreadsheets and started spending their time actually advising clients. Every workflow is designed around the same principle: if something is predictable, it shouldn't require a human to do it.
